Student Loan Default Insurance for Higher Education Institutions
Are you concerned about your former students defaulting on their student loan payments?

Cohort default rates have been falling since the Great Recession ended, but the future is unknown. The next recession may always be just around the corner, which could put your institution at risk for rising cohort default rates.

When cohort default rates rise high enough, your institution could face sanctions from the U.S. Department of Education.

Edu$afe's Institutional Product
Edu$afe offers a product that holistically protects both the university and its students from student loan defaults during the crucial three-year cohort default rate measurement period. The product has two features:

  • Unenrollment Warranty Coverage (UWC)
  • Undergraduate Unemployment Insurance (UUI)
Unenrollment Warranty Coverage (UWC)
This feature is a warranty that will pay up to 36 months of student loan payments for those former students of your institution that are most vulnerable to default: namely, those that unenrolled within their first or second year without completing their education.

So long as the former student enters an income-based repayment (IBR) plan, Edu$afe will cover their student loan payments for up to 36 months.

Undergraduate Unemployment Insurance (UUI)
This feature of Edu$afe’s product will cover up to 36 months of student loan payments for graduates of your institution that cannot find employment after actively searching for 3 months. A claim for this coverage can be made any time within 1 year following graduation.

This feature protects the student and the university against the unexpected event of graduation occurring during a recession.
