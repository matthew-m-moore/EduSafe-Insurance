Unemployment Insurance for Future College Graduates
Do you find yourself fretting over whether you'll be able to find a decent job once you obtain your new college degree?


Are you ever nervous that your choice of major could suddenly be less worthwhile in today's job market?


Do you have real concerns about how you'll afford to pay your debt after furthering your education?


Well, we here at Edu$afe have you covered. Click below to estimate your monthly payment.




Want to know more about how Edu$afe's product works? Keep scrolling to find out.
Step 1
We calculate your monthly payments based on the following information:
  • The type of school you attend, public or private, for example.
  • Your choice of major, or you can pick "Undeclared".
  • The date you started school.
  • Your expected graduation date.
  • The annual salary you expect to make after graduation.
You pick the coverage level you want. You can choose as little as 1 month of salary or as much as 1 year.

Step 2
You pay Edu$afe monthly payments up until your graduation date. Your payments may change once a year based on:
  • Your grades
  • Declaring or changing your major
  • Your participation in sports, clubs, etc.
  • Any internships you get
  • Changes in the economy
If you drop out of school, you can be eligible to get up to 50% of your total payments back.

Step 3
After graduation, if you cannot get a job after three months of trying, you can make a claim. Simple as that.

If you choose to go to grad school or line up a job before graduating, you can get up to 25% of your total payments back.
